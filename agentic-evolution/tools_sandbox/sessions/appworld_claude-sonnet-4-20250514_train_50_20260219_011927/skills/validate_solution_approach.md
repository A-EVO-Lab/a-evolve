# validate_solution_approach

**Scope:** 📚 Strategic Skill

**Description:** Validate that your planned solution approach will semantically satisfy the task requirements before implementing it

**When to use:** before implementing any solution, after planning but before execution, when you've identified a potential shortcut or workaround, for any task with ambiguous or quality-based requirements

## Instructions

Step 1: State your planned solution approach in one sentence.

Step 2: Identify the core mechanism of your approach:
- Are you looping/repeating content?
- Are you providing distinct content?
- Are you using a workaround or a comprehensive solution?

Step 3: Compare against task requirements:
- Does your approach provide what the task literally asks for?
- Does your approach match typical user intent for this type of task?
- Would a user be satisfied with this approach?

Step 4: Red flags to check:
- ⚠️ Using 'loop' or 'repeat' for tasks requiring variety
- ⚠️ Single playlist for long-duration requirements
- ⚠️ Shortcuts that technically work but provide poor experience
- ⚠️ Ignoring implicit quality requirements (variety, appropriateness)

Step 5: If any red flags are present, revise your approach:
- Replace looping with queueing multiple items
- Replace single items with multiple diverse items
- Replace shortcuts with comprehensive solutions

Step 6: Document your validated approach and proceed with confidence.

Step 7: If you're uncertain whether your approach is appropriate, choose the more comprehensive solution (it's better to over-deliver than under-deliver).
